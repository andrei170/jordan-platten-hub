Content Strategy - Jordan Platten

Frame: Once the three install layers are live, the content engine that already exists (YouTube long-form + IG + podcast + book funnel) starts routing into the new surfaces (scorecard, cohort, Academy VSL). This is the content cadence that lights up post-install.

Hook bank (40 hooks)

Each hook is shaped for one of three platforms: YouTube long-form (Y), IG / TikTok short-form (R), Podcast or LinkedIn long-form (P). Each hook is one sentence. Use as opening 5-15 seconds.

  1. (R) "If you're under £10K/mo with an agency, here's the 12-question diagnostic I'd run before I changed anything."

Long-form (YouTube) plan

Cadence: 1 long-form per week, 20-35 min, education-first (same as current).

Cohort module walkthrough mini-series (4 videos, post-install)

Once the cohort has run its first cycle, film a 4-video mini-series on the channel. One video per module, 25-30 min each. Use the framing "What we actually covered in the cohort + the takeaway the room landed on." Plays to the warm YouTube viewer as both proof of the cohort and indoctrination for the next cycle.

  • Video 1: Module 1 walkthrough - Acquisition rebuild
  • Video 2: Module 2 walkthrough - Delivery + retention
  • Video 3: Module 3 walkthrough - Hiring + ops cadence
  • Video 4: Module 4 walkthrough - Cash flow + price-ladder

Scorecard-driven evergreen (3-4 videos)

Once the Agency Scale Score is live, film 3-4 evergreen long-forms that route directly to it. Each long-form takes one of the 12 scorecard questions and turns it into a 25-minute teach. The video CTA is "Run the full 12-question version yourself, link in description."

Short-form (IG / TikTok) plan

Cadence: 3-5 reels per week, 30-90 seconds each.

Reel format 1 · Cohort behind-the-scenes (post-install)

Live footage from the in-person London 2 days. Operator on the whiteboard, room reactions, one-line operator takeaway. 30-45 sec. CTA: "Next cohort cycle opens [DATE]."

Reel format 2 · Scorecard insight (rolling)

One scorecard question + the 30-second teach on it. CTA: "Run the full 12-question version on the link in bio."

Reel format 3 · Alumni snapshot (post-Phase 3)

One cohort alumnus filmed in their own office, 60-90 sec, three numbers and one quote. Same format as existing "From £0 to £30K/mo - meet [name]" case-study posts.

Reel format 4 · Operator-tone short take (rolling)

Direct-to-camera, 30 sec, one observation about the £10-30K/mo agency stage. No CTA, brand-equity play.

Podcast plan

Cadence: 1-2 episodes per week (same as current).

Cohort-anchored episodes (3-4 in the first 90 days post-install)

  • Episode A: "Why I built the Operator Cohort instead of scaling the Academy curriculum"
  • Episode B: "What an operator-cohort week actually looks like, behind the scenes"
  • Episode C: "Three things the cohort taught me about the £10-50K/mo operator that I didn't know running Affluent.co"
  • Episode D: Cohort alumnus interview (post-90-day implementation window)

Photoshoot brief

One-day shoot. London-area studio or Affluent.co office.

Outputs: - Hero shot for Operator Cohort landing page (Jordan in operator-tone, deep-black backdrop, gold accent lighting, 16:9 + 4:5 + 9:16 ratios) - Academy VSL thumbnail (mid-sentence frame, gold accent overlay) - 4-6 reel B-roll cuts (Jordan walking, on a call, at a whiteboard) - Podcast cover refresh (optional) - Cohort venue B-roll (separate shoot post-first-cohort)

Wardrobe: Operator register. No suit. No graphic tee. Plain black or charcoal. Aligns with existing Affluent.co brand language.

Palette: Deep black background, gold accent lighting, cream / off-white skin-tone neutralisers.
