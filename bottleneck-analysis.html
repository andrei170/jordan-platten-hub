Bottleneck Analysis - Jordan Platten

Frame: Cold first-touch, install-only register. AG1 is the execution operator who could ship the three pieces below into Jordan's own house without competing with the Academy curriculum or the Agency Launch funnel. No strategy redo. No teaching. Just execution capacity for the layers that take a week of operator time Jordan would rather spend on the content engine.

The three things, ranked by revenue impact

01 · The Academy buy page does not embed a VSL or a close-window waitlist capture

Tag: Highest Revenue Lever · Fast Install

The Affluent Academy 3.0 sales page on members.affluent.co is well-built. Curriculum bullets are tight. The buy CTA sits at ~$947 / £757. The Trustpilot 4.8 alumni wall lives on Trustpilot, the YouTube long-form catalogue lives on YouTube, and the buy page is its own surface.

What the buy page does not currently carry on the surface: a 5-7 minute embedded VSL that opens the cold viewer into the Academy curriculum in the first 60 seconds, and a 30-second waitlist capture that runs during any closed-cart window. The friction-averse buyer who hits the page warm off a 26-minute YouTube long-form and would convert on a short indoctrination video sees a curriculum list and a buy button. The buyer who hits a closed-cart window has no email-list bridge into the next open window.

Install version: 5-7 minute Academy VSL filmed once, edited, hosted, transcript-backed, embedded on the buy page next to the existing CTA. Waitlist capture form that auto-fires during any close window and routes into a segmented email sequence (book buyer / cold viewer / Launch alumnus). No replacement of the curriculum bullets. No replacement of the buy button. Adds two surfaces to a working page.

Cost of waiting: ~£3-10K/mo in friction-averse Academy conversions that close on a VSL but bounce off the curriculum-list-only page. ~£40-120K/yr on the academy line alone. Six months from now: ~£20-60K never collected, plus the closed-cart windows in that period have no captured waitlist to launch into. Fix: 5-7 minute Academy VSL + waitlist capture surface on the academy buy page. Year-1 floor £40K-£120K incremental.

02 · ~350K YouTube subscriber base with no scorecard surface segmenting by stage

Tag: Compound Pickup · Fast Install

The YouTube channel is the warmest top-of-funnel asset. 26-minute average video length, education-first cadence, "How To Scale Your SMMA" / "New SMMA Offers" / "Service Delivery" catalogue. The bio routes to jordanplatten.co.uk and to affluent.academy.

The affluent.academy router asks "What is your current monthly revenue level?" and sends the viewer into one of three call-funnel destinations (agency-launch.ai, eliteagency.ai, agencyaccession.com). The routing is correct. What is missing is the email-capturing version of the same question that lives upstream of the call funnel and that segments the warm viewer into a tracked list before they ever hit a sales call.

Install version: 12-question "Agency Scale Score" on a single page. Returns a 0-100 readiness score, a 3-line bottleneck readout, captures email, segments by stage (0-10K, 10K-100K, 100K+) and by niche. The score makes the warm viewer feel diagnosed, not pitched. The email capture turns the viewer from "watched once on YouTube" into "tracked subscriber, segmented, on a nurture sequence." The 0-10K segment routes into the Academy waitlist + Academy VSL. The 10K-100K segment routes into the operator cohort (item 03). The 100K+ segment routes into the existing Accession funnel.

Cost of waiting: ~£5-15K/mo in subscriber + ascension revenue that compounds for the life of the list. ~£60-180K/yr on the segmented nurture line. Six months from now: ~£30-90K never captured. The list is 6 months smaller than it should be when the next Academy open-cart window or cohort intake lands. Fix: Agency Scale Score (ScoreApp / Typeform) + segmented 14-day nurture per stage. Year-1 floor £60K-£180K incremental. Compounds for life of the list.

03 · No publicly priced operator cohort surface between the £757 Academy and the £100K/mo Accession floor

Tag: Highest Revenue Lever · Public-Priced Layer

The £757 Academy is the right product for the 0-10K operator. The application-gated Agency Launch (~£3-4K) is the right product for the 0-10K operator ready for high-touch. The Accession mastermind is the right product for the £100K/mo operator. The gap is the £10K-£100K/mo operator who has graduated the Academy curriculum, is not yet at the Accession floor, and currently has nowhere to ascend without first booking a sales call into the Launch funnel.

This is the warmest list segment for any agency-education brand. Academy graduates plus Launch alumni who hit £10K/mo are the segment most likely to renew, refer, and upsell. Right now the only ascension path is a setter call into Launch, which converts on a different shape of operator than the £10K-£100K/mo founder typically wants.

Install version: "Agency Operator Cohort" landing page, public-priced at £15K-£20K, 4-6 seats per cohort, quarterly intakes, 4-minute application, direct enrolment on submit (no setter required). Cohort format: 2-day London-area in-person + 90-day implementation. Filter: Academy graduate or Launch alumnus, agency at £8K+/mo or 5+ retained clients. Sits next to the existing Academy + Launch funnel without dismantling either.

Cost of waiting: ~£30-80K per cohort cycle in seats that do not exist. 4 cycles a year x 4-6 seats x £15K-£20K = £240K-£480K/yr on the cohort line alone. Six months from now: ~£120-240K in cohort revenue never collected. Two cohort cycles missed. The warmest list segment stays unmonetised at the operator-cohort tier. Fix: Public-priced cohort landing page + 4-minute application Typeform + direct enrolment. Year-1 floor £240K-£480K direct.

Why these three, in this order

The ordering is install impact, not ego.

01 ships first because the Academy buy page is the most-visited paid surface in the funnel, and adding a VSL plus a waitlist capture is the highest-leverage one-week build in the system. It does not require any new product, just a new surface on an existing page.

02 ships second because it is the load-bearing piece for everything else. Without the scorecard, the ascension into the cohort (item 03) has no warm-list mechanism. With the scorecard, the cohort fills off email instead of off content-by-content acquisition.

03 ships in the same window because the operator-cohort tier captures the warmest list segment Jordan already owns. Once the scorecard exists, the cohort fills itself from the list.

Phases 3-4 (content compound, paid traffic) are post-install scaling layers. They run on Jordan's timeline, not the install clock.

What this build does NOT touch

  • Academy curriculum. The curriculum is the product. AG1 does not redesign it.
  • Agency Launch sales process. The Launch call funnel and the "Sign 10 clients or you don't pay" guarantee are Jordan's wedge. AG1 does not rewrite them.
  • Affluent.co DTC agency. Different lane entirely. AG1 does not pitch into it.
  • Appointwise / Clay / Skool / TripleWhale. Software investments. Not in scope.

The three install layers slot between the existing surfaces. They do not replace anything.
